Literature Survey

The increasing trend in pet ownership, especially among dogs, has led to a rising demand for advanced pet care solutions. Traditional methods of pet care, such as frequent veterinary visits, can be stressful, time-consuming, and costly for pet owners, leading to a need for innovative mobile-based solutions [1]. In recent years, Machine Learning (ML) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) have become essential tools in healthcare, proving their effectiveness in diagnosing medical conditions that are difficult for humans to detect manually. For instance, studies have demonstrated that CNN-based models achieve over 90% accuracy in identifying diseases from medical images [2].
Similarly, AR technology has shown significant potential in medical applications, where it enhances data collection accuracy and improves diagnostics [3]. According to Herath et al. [2], AR combined with CNN models can provide real-time analysis and identification of health conditions, which can be integrated into mobile applications for pet care management.
This project proposes the use of advanced CNN models like DenseNet121 and Xception, combined with AR technology, to develop a mobile application that assists dog owners in diagnosing skin conditions, assessing body conditions, and identifying breeds. The mobile application will also offer tailored feeding plans, exercise routines, and behavior analysis to ensure comprehensive pet care management [1].
References:

Research Gap

Identification & Classification

While there are several mobile applications for pet health, there is a lack of comprehensive solutions that integrate real-time skin condition identification, breed classification, and body condition analysis for pets, particularly dogs. Most applications focus on one or two aspects but fail to provide a holistic tool that addresses multiple health parameters simultaneously. Additionally, many current solutions do not incorporate advanced technologies like Augmented Reality (AR) to assist users in accurately capturing and diagnosing skin conditions, which is crucial for effective treatment recommendations.

Severity Assessment

Although smart solutions for pet care exist in some regions, there is limited research focusing on the severity assessment of pet health conditions, such as the progression of skin diseases or body condition issues. Current applications can detect conditions but often fail to assess their severity and progression over time, which is important for timely and appropriate interventions.

Information Sharing

There is a significant gap in real-time communication between pet owners, veterinarians, and healthcare providers. While some apps offer diagnostic tools, they lack features for continuous information sharing that would enable effective follow-up treatments and progress monitoring. Integrating a communication system that allows for seamless interaction between pet owners and professionals could significantly improve pet health management and the overall well-being of animals.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

How can we effectively classify and monitor pet health issues, such as skin conditions, weight management, and behavioral changes, while providing real-time surveillance to pet owners and veterinarians?

APets, especially dogs, are vulnerable to various health problems like skin infections, weight imbalances, and abnormal behaviors. Without timely identification and intervention, these issues can worsen, impacting the overall well-being of pets. Early detection of health issues, coupled with efficient communication between pet owners and veterinarians, is crucial for managing these conditions effectively and preventing severe health complications. Therefore, an intelligent system is needed to provide real-time health monitoring and diagnostics, allowing for early intervention and better health outcomes for pets.

Research Solution

"PAWSAGE" is a mobile app designed to help dog owners manage their pet’s health using Deep Learning and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) across three main components: skin condition identification, nutrition management, and activity monitoring.

The app uses CNN models to identify skin issues like bacterial and fungal infections through AR-guided image capture. It ensures early detection and provides treatment progress while tracking the skin's health over time.

For nutrition management, the CNN model assesses a dog's body condition (underweight, ideal, overweight) and offers personalized feeding and exercise plans, prompting vet consultation for severe cases.

For activity monitoring, YOLO v8 analyzes video footage to track activity levels and detect abnormal behavior. Real-time alerts and progress charts help owners adjust routines to maintain their dog's health.

This comprehensive system ensures proactive pet care, keeping owners informed and their pets healthy.

Research Objectives

1

Dog Skin Conditions Management:

The first objective is to classify various dog skin conditions using a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). This will provide a solution for distinguishing common skin conditions such as bacterial infections, fungal infections, and allergic reactions from healthy skin. In addition, the severity of each skin condition will be determined to inform pet owners about the appropriate treatments and precautions to reduce complications.

2

Dog Nutrition Management:

The second objective of this study is to develop a solution to identify a dog's breed and body condition (underweight, ideal, overweight) by analyzing images. The system will help pet owners monitor their dog’s health and provide personalized care suggestions based on the pet's breed and body condition, thus supporting overall health management.

3

Dog Behavior Monitoring:

The third objective is to monitor and analyze a dog’s behavior to detect changes in physical and emotional well-being. This will involve tracking various activities like movement, tail wagging, and sleep patterns. Real-time analysis of behavior will help in the early detection of potential health issues or discomfort, allowing pet owners to address these problems more quickly.

Methodology

The proposed pet care system comprises three main components:

1. Skin Condition Identification
2. Body Condition, Breed, and Life Stage Identification
3. Behavior Monitoring and Activity Analysis

The system is built using React-Native for the frontend, Node.js for the backend, and Firebase for data storage, with TensorFlow and Python for model training. The skin condition detection component uses AR-guided image capture along with a CNN for classification. For identifying body conditions, breeds, and life stages, a CNN model built on the Xception architecture is employed. Meanwhile, dog activity analysis is conducted through video data using the YOLOv8 algorithm.

In the Skin Condition Identification module, images collected from veterinary clinics and pet owners are used to train a model. This model classifies skin conditions such as bacterial infections, fungal infections, allergic conditions, and healthy states. AR technology assists users in capturing high-quality images, which are then processed to provide classification results along with treatment recommendations. Users can also track their pets' skin health over time by capturing weekly images, with progress data available for veterinarians.

In the Body Condition, Breed, and Life Stage Identification module, images of dogs from different life stages and body conditions are used to train a fine-tuned Xception model. This component identifies the dog's breed, predicts its life stage, and assesses its body condition. Based on the body condition, the system suggests feeding plans and provides workout videos tailored to help the dog maintain or achieve an ideal weight.

The Behavior Monitoring module analyzes video data to detect various dog behaviors such as walking style and tail wagging patterns. A custom video dataset is processed using the YOLOv8 algorithm to track dogs in real-time, isolating their behavior for analysis. The system categorizes the dog's condition as good or bad, based on posture and movement patterns, and alerts users if the dog is in poor condition, providing immediate veterinarian contact information when necessary.
